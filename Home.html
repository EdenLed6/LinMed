Our Devices

Bellavista

The BellaVista ventilator provides the same high level of ventilation found in intensive care units. It supports all forms of ventilation (both invasive and non-invasive) offering some of the highest diagnostic and performance capabilities in the world. Its lightweight and user-friendly design make it ideal for home care as well as for transporting ventilated patients. The advanced technology and innovative design ensure ease of use, while the low maintenance costs make it an economical choice.

PhysioFlow® Family of Products

The PhysioFlow® family of products includes a range of non-invasive devices designed to measure cardiac output. These devices provide continuous, reliable, and accurate hemodynamic metrics, comparable to invasive methods. They are particularly effective for patients where traditional cardiac output measurement is difficult, such as those with obesity, pulmonary edema, or emphysema. Uniquely, the PhysioFlow® devices deliver reliable and accurate measurements even during strenuous activity or intensive care, scenarios where such metrics were previously unattainable. The devices are non-invasive, user-friendly, and offer consistent, precise measurements across various medical conditions and locations.

Ventway Sparrow

The portable ventilator is designed for ventilated patients on the move, including transport by ambulance, car, or wheelchair for physically limited patients. The device is lightweight, durable, and user-friendly, providing excellent ventilation capabilities.

Hayek

The Hayek RTX offers an efficient and effective option for positive pressure ventilation by operating with negative pressure ventilation, which is non-invasive and thus a true alternative to traditional ventilation methods. Its BCV technology provides dual-mode ventilation, allowing active control over the breathing phases (inhalation and exhalation) in a physiological manner, thereby enhancing the user's cardiac output with proven efficiency and success. The device is non-invasive, mimics natural breathing, enhances cardiac output, facilitates respiratory physiotherapy, and provides continuous care without the need for additional personnel.
